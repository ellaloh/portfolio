Competitive Analysis

I analyzed two competitor apps to identify underserved opportunities in the market, spot weaknesses in their user experience, and make better-informed decisions about the products’ overall strategy

Rooftop Slushie
Doctor on Demand

Rooftop Slushie is a career advice service geared toward technical interviews. Experts from the world’s leading companies provide trustworthy answers and users can comfortably ask questions.

They are a direct competitor that also offers other services that have grown bigger than their career advice feature like paid referrals.

See a board-certified, US-based doctor, 24/7. It is widely used by millions of patients, trusted widely, and has affordable and transparent pricing.

Also not a direct competitor but has garnered a large network of users and experts.

Survey Results

Key users and stakeholders were interviewed with each iteration and before even moving a pixel.

Key Insights

People on average, prep for interviews by themselves but use a mix of mediums to get the information and still the information is not complete.

People want inside information from HR or employees currently at the company they're interviewing for.

70% of respondents said they would call a mentor atleast once a week.

Usability Testing

Methodology used

Remote and in-person moderated

Analysis

An affinity map was created to categorize findings into observations, positive quotes, negative quotes, and errors. Then, a Spreadsheet organized the information collected through affinity mapping into visual patterns.

Issues

I identified the top five errors and addressed them in the next prototype.

Test Results & Solutions

These 4 (out of 5) issues were prioritized based on severity level and frequency in which they occured for the next round of iterations.

Issue 1


"
The cost to call an expert is not reliable: $$(20+), average call $30, but when you book the mentor, $50. What is the $-$$$ range.
"

Suggested Change

Change $$ iconography to a range and a specific dollar amount when booking.

Evidence

3 out of 6 participants mentioned this issue.

Issue 2


"
I thought I would be able to call Elise from the ‘Meetings’ tab.
"

Suggested Change

Add clickable area to call mentor from calendar event. Add video and call options to make it clear.

Evidence

3 out of 6 participants mentioned this issue.

Issue 3


"
Users don’t immediately see the save icon but they see the word ‘save’. They try to click on the other icons that display only information, and are not actionable.
"

Suggested Change


Enlarge ‘Save’ icon, take out other non-clickable icons, leave text and move to the upper right hand corner where it usually is. Add pictures for less distraction.

Evidence

2 out of 6 participants mentioned this issue.

Issue 4


"
Users think the text ‘connect’ is a link to sign-up.
 "

Suggested Change

Create a clear hierarchy of text.

Evidence

2 out of 6 participants mentioned this issue.

Future Steps

New Hypothesis

A seamless design doesn’t make the user think twice about what they should do next. Users are highly technical and understand how an app should work, thus the app should follow trending UI patterns and graphics. Furthermore, the app is now past the MVP stage, but needs to focus on the Mentoring side of the app (login, build profiles, interact with clients).

Hypothesis Validation

At the moment, usability fixes based on testing needs to be put aside while, accessibility updates, A/B testing, and updates for responsive and native design are needed.
